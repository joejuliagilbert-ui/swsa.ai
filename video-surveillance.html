Commercial Video Surveillance Solutions

Southwest Security & Automation helps commercial properties plan and install video surveillance systems that match the way the site actually operates. The goal is useful camera coverage, clean installation, practical access to footage, and a system that can be supported after the work is done.

We focus on the real details: where people enter, where vehicles move, where staff need visibility, where blind spots exist, how lighting changes after hours, and how the system will be used day to day.

What Video Surveillance Can Cover

  • Entrances & Exits including front doors, staff entries, delivery points, and controlled access areas
  • Parking Lots & Exterior Areas where visibility can help document activity around the property
  • Warehouses & Work Areas including loading zones, inventory areas, service bays, and yards
  • Customer & Staff Areas for retail spaces, offices, counters, lobbies, and public-facing areas

Camera Planning & Placement

Good camera placement matters more than simply adding more cameras. We look at sightlines, mounting height, glare, nighttime visibility, network access, power needs, and whether each camera will produce footage that is actually useful.

For many properties, the right plan includes a mix of entry coverage, exterior views, parking visibility, interior common areas, and key operational areas. We can also help identify where future cameras or access control points may make sense as the property changes.

Remote Visibility & Monitoring

Remote visibility can help owners, managers, and authorized staff check important areas when they are away from the site. Depending on the system design, this may include mobile app access, multi-site viewing, alerts, and after-hours visibility options.

Monitoring needs vary by property, equipment, budget, and risk level. We keep the conversation practical and make sure expectations match the system being installed.

AI Video Analytics

AI-supported video analytics can be useful when the property, camera placement, and equipment are a good fit. These tools may help improve alert quality, support smarter detection, and reduce unnecessary notifications, but they work best when planned carefully.

We can help evaluate where analytics may make sense, which areas should stay simple, and how to plan a system that can support future upgrades without overbuilding on day one.
