Commercial Access Control Solutions

Southwest Security & Automation helps commercial properties plan access control around real doors, gates, staff movement, restricted areas, and daily operations. The goal is a practical system that improves control over who can enter important spaces without making the property harder to operate.

Every site is different. We look at the property layout, how people move through the building, where public access should stop, where staff need access, and where video coverage or alarm coverage may also be useful.

Doors, Gates & Controlled Entry Points

  • Door Access Control for staff entries, office doors, shared entrances, restricted rooms, and service areas
  • Controlled Entry Points for lobbies, tenant spaces, managed buildings, and public-to-private transition areas
  • Gate & Yard Access for warehouses, industrial properties, service yards, and vehicle entry points
  • Access Planning to decide which doors need control, which areas should stay simple, and where future expansion may make sense

Staff Areas, Offices & Restricted Spaces

Access control is often most useful where a property has staff-only spaces, inventory areas, records rooms, leasing offices, equipment rooms, or areas that should not be open to every visitor or employee. A practical plan helps separate public, staff, and restricted spaces clearly.

For many properties, the best starting point is a small number of high-value doors or gates. From there, the system can be planned so additional controlled entry points can be added later if the site grows or the security needs change.

Access Control Planning & Installation

A good access control project starts with questions about the property, not a generic equipment list. We look at entry points, door hardware, gate needs, network access, power requirements, user management, staff routines, and how the system will be supported after installation.

The right design depends on the building and the way people use it. We keep expectations clear and avoid overbuilding when a simpler access plan will solve the real problem.

Video Surveillance Integration

Access control and video surveillance often work best when planned together. Cameras can provide visibility around doors, gates, parking areas, delivery points, and restricted spaces so managers have better context when reviewing activity.

If video coverage is part of the project, we can plan access control alongside commercial video surveillance so the system is easier to understand and expand over time.
